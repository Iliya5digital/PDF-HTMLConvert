Perfect Mix Checklist
1. Levels & Cleaning
❏I’m not clipping on the master. If master is clipping - delete all master plugins (if
any) and bring down the master fader.
❏My levels are set properly. The major elements - vocal, drums, melody - are
clearly audible and I can easily focus on them. They are not taken over by the
minor elements (fxs, background crashes, noises, miscellaneous percussion)
❏Every element has its purpose in the track. There are no unnecessary instruments
which don’t bring any value but only messes up the mix.
❏EQ - all instruments have their own space in the mix spectrum, there are no layers
of the sub frequencies (sub bass/808, kick) and there are as few bass layers as
possible - to prevent muddy mix. Bass has a short release to not overlap.
❏Mid Side EQ is being used on instrument groups to make sure there is no
muddiness in low instruments.
❏Frequencies that every single element is not focused on - are cut, and every
group but kick & bass are low cut at at least 140hz (or 500hz if possible)
2. Dynamics
❏Compression - There are no overwhelming and unnecessary volume peaks of all
the elements of the song. Crashes, percussion, or other effects are not popping
through the mix more than the major elements. If necessary every element is
controlled with even subtle compression, in order not to let it be audible more
than indented.
❏Optional Drum Buss parallel compression for punch.
❏Drum and Bass group sounds glued together - if not, use subtle
saturation/distortion plugin Multiband Compressor with slight setting on all
bands

3. Dry & Wet Effects
❏Wet FXs - Reverb and delay on the vocal are sidechained to its dry signal (one
click solution with our free Patcher preset). Not only vocal but also all the
elements that need the clarity (eg. melody). Use Self Sidechained Reverb from
Patcher Lords Preset Pack
❏Percussion, especially snare drum, has turned on delay of the reverb to ~50ms (if
reverb was used) to keep the transients clear and not blurry.
❏Properly EQ’d Vocal - low cut for no “podcast feel”, soft high boosted for
presence if necessary & de-ess’d to take care of harsh high frequencies.
❏Vocal is compressed to make every word equally audible. It’s dynamic but not
randomly peaky. Best resolved with compression & soft limiting after.
❏Vocal layers:
- are perfectly in time with the main vocal
- don’t have any reverb (unless totally necessary)
- high cut applied, to make the main lyrics transparent & clear
- low cut applied to keep the focus on main vocal
- if made from the exact same recording as main are richly processed,
(no wet fxs, distorted and stereo widened)
- are more compressed, to keep the main vocal more popping
❏Vocal as it’s the most important element - is the loudest, lyrics are clearly
audible.
❏Sidechain from Vocal to Instruments Buss - the best multiband sidechain
patcher preset applied (we do it in the House chapter of Mixing Course). In order
to dynamically create space in the instrument section for the vocal.
❏The lower in the frequency spectrum - the more mono my elements are.
Frequencies and instruments that play under 100 hz are fully in mono
FLTips.com
4. Finalizing
❏Surgical EQ - there are no overwhelmingly harsh frequency peaks in any element
of the track. Especially happening in melodies, vocal, FXs & real recordings or
loops.
❏Panning - Panning is varied & automated - even randomly - to bring excitement
and enrich the stereo spectrum - useful tool Fruity Panomatic. Especially in hats,
percussion and other miscellaneous elements.
❏Mono Compatibility - if any element was widened - it was done with a plugin
which prevents the right and left channel from Phase Issues. The plugin was
placed before any wet effects in the mixer channel. (Use iZotope Ozone Imager
or our one of our Presets from Patcher Lords Pack)
❏There is an audible difference between breakdown and drop in volume. The drop
is the loudest part of my song. If not - automate volume through the whole track
in +/- 4db range.
❏Stereo & Mono is automated throughout the track to introduce contrast between
breakdown and drop. Master Channel Stereo is automated (e.g. using Fruity
Stereo Enhancer) to merge into 40-30% mono in the buildup just before drop and
released to neutral mode on the drop.
❏My Master channel is empty - ready for rendering for mastering. (Mastering
continuation in the Mastering Course - Perfect Mastering Checklist)
